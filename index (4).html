your 6 rules
01
1 workout every day 45–75 min
Gym 5x/week (strength focus) + 2 active recovery days — walk, yoga, Pilates, swim. At least 3 lifting sessions per week. No full rest days. Active rest counts.
02
3 liters of water daily before 9pm
Not the gallon of 75 Hard — 3L is transformative for your skin, bloating, energy, and appetite. A marked water bottle makes this automatic.
03
Follow your eating plan 6 days clean
High protein (0.8g per lb bodyweight), whole foods, slight deficit on rest days. 1 flexible meal Sunday — not a cheat day. Zero alcohol for the full 75 days.
04
10 pages of reading daily non-fiction
No phone reading. Kindle or physical book. Pick something genuinely interesting — personal finance, self-development, whatever excites you. 10 pages = ~15 mins.
05
No phone before 10am or after 9pm dopamine rule
Morning phone use hijacks your cortisol and kills focus for hours. Evening use destroys sleep quality, skin repair, and mental clarity. These windows are yours now.
06
5-min evening journal + 1 thing learned learning rule
Every night: 3 wins from today, 1 thing you genuinely learned, tomorrow's top 3 priorities. This closes your day intentionally instead of scrolling until you pass out.
The 75 Medium miss rule
Miss a rule → don't restart. Log it honestly, write one sentence about why, and go harder tomorrow. Consistency over perfection. The girl who shows up 70 out of 75 days still wins.

why you're doing this
🎓
Walking back to campus different
75 days from now you'll be stepping back onto campus with a body you built, a mind you sharpened, and energy nobody can take from you. That girl turns heads not because she tried to — but because she just did the work.
💪
The revenge body is really for you
Forget everyone else. This is about walking into a room and feeling at home in your body. The confidence that comes from keeping promises to yourself is something no one can give you and no one can take away.
💰
Rich girl habits start now
Rich women are disciplined women. They wake up early. They invest in themselves. They don't numb out on their phones for 6 hours. The financial reading and learning habit you build this summer is the foundation of who you'll be at 25, 30, 35.
🧘
Your nervous system is resetting
The no-phone windows feel hard for the first 2 weeks because your dopamine system is genuinely addicted to the constant stimulation. Push through it. By week 4 you'll feel calmer, sharper, and more present than you have in years.
✨
The glow is real, not metaphorical
3L of water daily + 7–8 hours sleep (the no-phone rule makes this happen) + clean eating = actual visible skin changes within 3–4 weeks. People will ask what you're doing differently. You won't have to say anything.
🏆
Discipline is the most attractive thing
Not the body. Not the clothes. The way you carry yourself when you know you've earned it. When you've been waking up before everyone else and doing the work while they're still scrolling — that energy is unmistakable.
📖
The girl who reads is dangerous
10 pages a day is 750 pages in 75 days — that's 2–3 entire books. Most college students don't read a single non-assigned book all year. The knowledge gap you build this summer compounds for the rest of your life.
🌙
Hard days are the point
The days you don't feel like going to the gym but go anyway — those are the days the identity shift happens. You're not building a body. You're building the identity of someone who shows up for herself no matter what.
